the productivity master 🌳

Secluded from the real world, a young student enters the Garden of Productivity, ready to learn the ways of the Master.

Master, how do I best optimize my workflow?

Have you made sure to use Obsidian?

No, Master. I'll do that right away.

Great. Now set up a Zettelkasten and go away until you've created 10,000 links between different notes.

A few months later, the student reports back to the master.

I've stored nearly everything I know into the Zettelkasten, Master.

Come back to me after you've started logging every minute for 30 days.

Do I track the time I spend combing my hair?

Once you do that, find out how much time you waste tying shoelaces.

Another month passes, and the student returns to the master.

Great. Remember that Zettelkasten? Folders are easier to search through, and they'll double your productivity. Come back when you've converted your Zettelkasten.

Halfway through reorganizing his notes, the student is agitated.

Master, your teachings aren't helping!

Then leave.

But you promised that I'd reach productivity enlightenment!

You imbecile! It takes time! How many books do you even read each month?

Twelve.

And you dare to think you can reach enlightenment?

How many should I be reading each month?

Eighty-two.

But there are only 24 hours in a day, and each book takes six hours to read!

Then listen to the audiobooks on triple speed.

Then I won't understand anything!

Train your ear. This is the only way to enlightenment.

But—the other students are reading just four!

And they're way closer to enlightenment than you despite that.

You make no sense! I've gained nothing from doing all this!

Say that again.

At that moment, the student was enlightened.
